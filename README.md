## Why Buy a VPS with Crypto? The Actual Reasons People Do This

## What Cryptocurrencies Do VPS Providers Actually Accept?

The most commonly supported options across crypto-friendly VPS providers in 2026 are:

- **Bitcoin (BTC)** — the baseline. Almost every provider that accepts crypto accepts BTC.
- **Ethereum (ETH)** — widely supported, faster confirmations than Bitcoin for most practical purposes.
- **USDT (Tether)** — stablecoins have become popular specifically because they eliminate the price volatility concern. USDT on the Tron (TRC20) network is particularly common because gas fees are low.
- **Litecoin (LTC)** — lower fees than BTC, still widely accepted.
- **Other altcoins** — some providers use payment processors like CoinPayments or Coinbase Commerce, which can open up 100+ additional currencies.

The key thing to check before committing: does the provider accept crypto **natively**, or are they routing through a third-party gateway that might add fees or have its own KYC requirements? Ideally you want a provider where the crypto payment is a first-class option, not an afterthought tacked on by a payment processor.

## What to Look for When You Buy VPS with Bitcoin

Before you go hunting for the cheapest Bitcoin VPS you can find, there are a few things worth thinking through:

**1. Actual performance, not just specs on paper**

The spec sheet can say anything. What you want is a provider whose CPU frequency claims hold up in independent benchmarks. Single-core performance matters enormously for web apps, bots, and most everyday workloads — and it varies wildly between providers even at the same price point.

**2. Location coverage**

Where are their data centers? If you're serving users in Southeast Asia and the only available nodes are in the US, you're going to have latency problems regardless of how fast the CPU is.

**3. Transparent pricing**

Some providers advertise a base price and then hit you with bandwidth overages, CPU burst fees, or IP surcharges. Read the fine print before you send that transaction.

**4. Refund policy for crypto**

Crypto payments are inherently non-reversible. If a provider offers a trial period or a 24-hour refund window for first-time customers, that's a meaningful safety net.

**5. Support quality**

If something goes wrong at 2am and your server is down, how do you reach them? Ticket-only support with multi-day response times is a different experience than a Telegram channel with fast turnarounds.
